Background
International Nomenclature Cosmetic Ingredient (INCI) names are uniform, systematic names internationally recognized to identify cosmetic ingredients. In the United States and many countries around the world, INCI names are referenced by regulation for ingredient labeling cosmetic products. INCI names are developed by the International Nomenclature Committee (INC) and published in the International Cosmetic Ingredient Dictionary and Handbook. The Dictionary, and on-line versions of the Dictionary, is available from the Personal Care Products Council’s e-bookstore: www.personalcarecouncil.org.

General Procedure
INCI names are designated by the International Nomenclature Committee (INC) through an application process. Applications must be filled out in English and submitted electronically. Only one trade name may be submitted per application, and there is a non-refundable fee of $250.00 per application payable by credit card upon submission of the completed application. Applicants may save their information during the filing process, however once the application is submitted it cannot be retrieved for editing.

INCI Application Processing
After an INCI application is submitted, the process for an INCI name assignment progresses through a series of steps:

  1. When payment is verified, the applicant is notified by email; the application is then initially reviewed for completeness.
  2. An email notification is sent to the applicant when the initial review has been performed.
  3. The application is again reviewed for adequate disclosure of composition and manufacturing information.
  4. An email notification is sent to the applicant if additional information is required.
  5. Applications determined to require full INC review are compiled in chronological order and distributed to INC members 6 weeks prior to the scheduled meeting date. Applicants are notified with this information.
  6. Applications that may be processed without full INC review are circulated among members as needed and assigned nomenclature.
  7. Applicants are notified from the INCI site by email with the assigned INCI name.

To ensure proper communication throughout the application process and avoid delays, it is imperative that your email server accepts mail from inci@personalcarecouncil.org.
